\begin{problem}
Prove that if $a \equiv b \pmod{14}$ and $a \equiv b \pmod{5}$, then $a \equiv b \pmod {70}$.

\begin{solution}
Unfolding the definitions, we know $a \equiv b \pmod{14}$ means $14 | a - b$, likewise $a \equiv b \pmod{5}$ implies $5 | a - b$. 
For any $p$, $q$ and $x$ we know if $p | x$ and $q | x$ and $p$ and $q$ are relatively prime, we know from class $pq | x$. So, applying that reasoning with $x = a - b$, $p = 14$ and $q = 5$ yields
$70 | a - b$, which by definition is what we were trying to prove.
\end{solution}

\end{problem}
