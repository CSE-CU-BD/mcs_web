\begin{problem}
Write out both a disjunctive and a conjunctive normal form for the proposition with truth table

\begin{tabular} {| l | c | c | c | r |}
  \hline
  A & B & C & D & ? \\ \hline
  T & T & T & T & T  \\ \hline
  T & T & T & F & F  \\ \hline
  T & T & F & T & T  \\ \hline
  T & T & F & F & F  \\ \hline
  T & F & T & T & T  \\ \hline
  T & F & T & F & T  \\ \hline
  T & F & F & T & T  \\ \hline
  T & F & F & F & T  \\ \hline
  F & T & T & T & T \\ \hline
  F & T & T & F & F  \\ \hline
  F & T & F & T & T  \\ \hline
  F & T & F & F & F  \\ \hline
  F & F & T & T & F  \\ \hline
  F & F & T & F & F  \\ \hline
  F & F & F & T & T  \\ \hline
  F & F & F & F & T  \\ \hline
\end{tabular}


\begin{solution}
We can see a conjunctive normal form as follows: we want all assignments that make P not be false not to be the case. We express this as an AND of ORs: one proposition per row of the truth table that makes the proposition false, and each of these propositions will be an OR of conditions that make that assignment not happen.
The result in this case is:

$
(\overline{A} \wedge \overline{B} \wedge \overline{C} \wedge {D}) \vee (\overline{A} \wedge \overline{B} \wedge {C} \wedge {D})
 \vee ({A} \wedge \overline{B} \wedge \overline{C} \wedge {D}) \vee ({A} \wedge \overline{B} \wedge {C} \wedge {D}) \vee ({A} \wedge {B} \wedge \overline{C} \wedge \overline{D}) \vee ({A} \wedge {B} \wedge \overline{C} \wedge {D})
$

Conversely, we can find a disjunctive normal form by considering the fact that at one of the rows that correspond to the proposition being true needs to be true for the proposition to be true. This yields an OR of ANDs. In this case, the result is:

$
({A} \vee {B} \vee {C} \vee {D}) \wedge ({A} \vee {B} \vee \overline{C} \vee {D}) \wedge ({A} \vee \overline{B} \vee {C} \vee {D}) \wedge ({A} \vee \overline{B} \vee {C} \vee \overline{D}) \wedge ({A} \vee \overline{B} \vee \overline{C} \vee {D}) \wedge ({A} \vee \overline{B} \vee \overline{C} \vee \overline{D}) \wedge (\overline{A} \vee {B} \vee {C} \vee {D}) \wedge (\overline{A} \vee {B} \vee \overline{C} \vee {D}) \wedge (\overline{A} \vee \overline{B} \vee \overline{C} \vee {D}) \wedge (\overline{A} \vee \overline{B} \vee \overline{C} \vee \overline{D})
$
\end{solution}

\end{problem}
