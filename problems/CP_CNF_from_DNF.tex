\begin{problem}
Explain how to find a conjunctive form for a propositional formula
directly from a disjunctive form for its complement.

\begin{solution}
If $D$ a disjunctive form equivalent to $\QNOT(F)$, then buy double
negation, $\QNOT(D)$ is equivalent to $F$.  So applying DeMorgan's Law
for \QNOT over \QOR~\bref{DeMQOR} to $\QNOT(D)$ will yield a
conjunctive form equivalent to $F$.
\end{solution}

\end{problem}
