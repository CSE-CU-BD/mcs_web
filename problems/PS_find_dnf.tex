\begin{problem}
\bparts

Use the equivalence axioms of
Section~\bref{propositional_equivalences_sec} to convert the formula
\[
A \QXOR B \QXOR C.
\]

\ppart \dots to disjunctive (\QOR\ of \QAND's) form,

\begin{solution}
\TBA{DERIVE USING ALGEBRAIC RULES}

Noting that $A \QXOR\ B \QXOR\ C$ evaluates to \true exactly when exactly
one of the inputs is \true or else all three are \true
\[\begin{array}{lr}
(A \QAND \bar{B} \QAND \bar{C}) & \QOR\\
(\bar{A} \QAND B \QAND \bar{C}) & \QOR\\
(\bar{A} \QAND \bar{B} \QAND C) & \QOR\\
(A \QAND B \QAND C).
\end{array}\]
\end{solution}

\ppart \dots to conjunctive (\QAND of \QOR's) form.

\begin{solution}
\TBA{DERIVE USING ALGEBRAIC RULES}

Using DeMorgan's theorem, and knowing that $A$ \QXOR\ $B$
\QXOR\ $C$ evaluates to \false\ exactly when any two of the
variables are \false\ or all of the variables are \false, we get a disjuntive normal form
\[\begin{array}{lr}
\bar{(A \QAND B \QAND \bar{C})} & \QAND\\
 \bar{(A \QAND \bar{B} \QAND C)} &\QAND\\
 \bar{(\bar{A} \QAND B \QAND C)} & \QAND\\
 \bar{(\bar{A} \QAND \bar{B} \QAND \bar{C})}
\end{array}\]
which converts to CNF
\[\begin{array}{lr}
(\bar{A} \QOR\ \bar{B} \QOR\ C) & \QAND\\
(\bar{A} \QOR\ B \QOR\ \bar{C}) & \QAND\\
(A \QOR\ \bar{B} \QOR\ \bar{C}) & \QAND\\
(\bar{A} \QOR\ \bar{B} \QOR\ \bar{C}).
\end{array}\]
\end{solution}

\eparts

\end{problem}
