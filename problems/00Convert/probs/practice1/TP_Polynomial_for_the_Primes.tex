\begin{problem}

%% type: short-answer
%% title: Polynomial for the Primes

Let $p(n) = n^2 + n + 41$. What is the smallest nonnegative integer
$n$ such that $p(n)$ is not prime?

\begin{solution}
40
\end{solution}
